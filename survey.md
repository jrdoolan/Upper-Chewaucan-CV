# Contingent Valuation Survey Instrument

**Project:** Upper Chewaucan Watershed Restoration (2025–2028)
**Administered by:** Joseph Doolan, EC434

---

## Recruitment Strategy & Limitations

Due to budget and time constraints, respondents were selected by convenience sampling. The survey was emailed to 60 individuals via Google Forms, yielding 31 responses.

**Known limitations:**
- Convenience sampling introduces bias; results cannot be generalized to the broader Oregon population
- Respondents skewed toward "outdoorsy" individuals
- Reported income may reflect personal rather than household income in some cases
- Small sample size (n = 31) limits statistical power

---

## Background

The **Lake County Umbrella Watershed Council (LCUWC)**, along with the **Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife (ODFW)** and the **Oregon Watershed Enhancement Board**, has proposed a project to restore the Upper Chewaucan Watershed in Lake County, Oregon.

The project focuses on:

1. Design fish passage improvement
2. Increase stream corridor habitat complexity
3. Enhance rangeland
4. Restore post-fire upland forest health

**Goal:** Improve the ecological function and resilience of the Upper Chewaucan Watershed.

Ecological degradation has been driven by droughts, grazing practices, infrastructure development, and wildfires. The species most impacted is the **Redband trout**, cut off from 71% of its historical habitat through fragmentation.
